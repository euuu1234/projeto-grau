About the club

The history of Vale de Ferro

Vale de Ferro was born from a simple and powerful idea: to turn work, unity, and pride into football. Founded by workers from a fictional industrial region, the club grew based on discipline, courage, and love for the shirt. What started as a factory team became a symbol of resistance and tradition.

How the club was formed

Vale de Ferro was created in 1987 by workers from an industrial area known as Vale de Ferro. At first, the team only played neighborhood matches and amateur tournaments between factories and communities in the region. The strength of the group came from the unity between friends, family members, and coworkers, who saw football as a way to represent their identity.

The early years

During the first years, the club faced simple fields, limited structure, and many challenges. Even so, the competitive spirit was never missing. Over time, the team began to stand out in local tournaments, drawing attention for its intensity on the field and for its ever-growing fan base.

The rise of the team

In the 2000s, Vale de Ferro went through a period of growth. New supporters, improved infrastructure, and a more solid sports project helped the club move up a level. From then on, the team was no longer just a regional promise and began competing in bigger competitions with real title ambitions.

The course of its history

1987 — Foundation The club emerges among workers and residents of the industrial region, with a simple uniform and a fighting spirit.
1990 — Regional consolidation Vale de Ferro begins to earn respect in local championships and creates its first rivalries.
2000 — Structure and growth The team becomes better organized, gains support, and starts competing in state tournaments with more strength.
2012 — Inauguration of Arena Forja The new stadium strengthens the club’s identity and turns matches into memorable events.
2015 to 2025 — Golden era Vale de Ferro becomes a powerhouse, wins important titles, and enters the elite of fictional football.

Club identity

  • Name: Vale de Ferro
  • Founded: 1987
  • Origin: fictional industrial region
  • Playing style: strength, intensity, and organization
  • Fan base: passionate, loud, and present at all times

Most Iconic Kits in Club History

Throughout its journey, Vale de Ferro built a strong visual identity through uniforms that became symbols of different eras. Some stood out for their modern look, others for bold color choices, and some were remembered for representing the roots of the club. Below are some of the most remarkable and memorable kits in Vale de Ferro’s history.

Black and Red Home Kit

The black and red home kit is the strongest symbol of Vale de Ferro’s identity. Its modern and aggressive design reflects the club’s strength, intensity, and competitive spirit. For many supporters, this is the uniform that best represents the team’s image on the field.

Blue Alternate Kit

The blue version became one of the most visually striking alternate kits in club history. It preserved the same design pattern as the main model, but introduced a cooler and more technical look, giving the team a different visual presence without losing its strong personality.

Yellow Alternate Kit

The yellow kit stood out for its vibrant and bold appearance. It became memorable for breaking away from the club’s traditional darker tones while still keeping the same design language. This model is often remembered as one of the most daring and energetic uniforms ever associated with Vale de Ferro.

1989 Retro Kit

Inspired by the club’s early years, the 1989 retro kit recalls the origins of Vale de Ferro and its working-class roots. With a simpler and more classic style, it represents tradition, memory, and the identity that shaped the club long before its rise to prominence. It is one of the most nostalgic uniforms in Vale de Ferro’s visual history.
